About the Violin

The violin is a string instrument that is played with a bow. It is the smallest and highest-pitched instrument in the string family. The violin has been used in classical music for centuries and is a popular instrument in many other genres as well.

Key features of the violin include its four strings, which are tuned in fifths, and its f-shaped sound holes, which help to project the sound of the instrument.
